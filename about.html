Emily's Story

Emily was born in Chicago, the greatest city in the world*. Sadly, about a year after she was born, her parents decided it would be better to raise children in the small town of Tecumseh. Having no say in the matter, Emily went along with the plan. She even allowed her parents to have some additional children; although, she was less than pleased when Walter was born without her presence. She made sure that didn't happen when Arthur was born. Emily quickly adjusted to living in rural Michigan, but the itch to return to the big city never went away and Emily moved back to Chicago after high school and attended DePaul University where she ran cross-country and track & field. After a brief relocation to Colorado, she spent 6 years teaching high school math and science at Chicago's Pritzker College Prep, after which she left the classroom in order to impact student achievement more broadly through a role at the KIPP Foundation. In her spare time, Emily enjoys convincing other people to run marathons, drinking sangria on rooftops, biking around the city, and tasting new beers. Also: science!

*According to Emily

Our Story

We first met in 2009 at the World's Largest Block Party. Despite a good first impression, it took another year for us to see each other again. From there, we spent several years commuting between Chicago and Kalamazoo, until 2014 when Mark moved in with Emily (and Sarah) in Chicago. Since then, we've enjoyed each other's company immensely while visiting the science museum, drinking beer on rooftops and running a Chicago marathon (though Mark has vowed to never run another one). To fill the void left by Sarah when she moved out, we recently adopted Sheffield: our terrified-of-the-world-but-deeply-loved rescue dog from PAWS.

Emily and Colleen

Emily and Colleen met in 2003 on the 21st of August when they moved into Seton Hall at DePaul University for pre-season cross-county. Colleen was wearing jean shorts, a blue and white t-shirt, and a baseball hat. Emily was wearing orange capris and a pink shirt. Since then, they have been classmates in courses like The Jewish Experience and Performing for the Non-Performance Major, watched every episode of Gilmore Girls and many episodes of Felicity (Emily often fell asleep), run countless miles together, eaten pounds of dried mango, and shared hundreds of "secrets". After 13 years of friendship, you can still find Emily and Colleen doing many of these things together, although the miles are fewer and slower. Or, you will find them on a couch drinking a bottle of wine.

Mark and Adam

We've been close friends ever since meeting as soccer teammates in 5th grade. Despite living 2000 miles away, we still find ways to visit each other for snowboarding, camping, or just to have a beer and catch up. As kids, we kept ourselves entertained with an endless stream of banter about nothing, that our parents didn't (and still don't) really care for. When that failed, well, that's when things got weird.

Emily and Sarah

Emily and Sarah met in 2010 when Emily was looking for a new place to live and Sarah was looking for a new roommate. The years living in the Warner apartment were eventful: there were parties with Pablo, a flooded basement, a turtle, snowpocalypse (which included snow angels and street volleyball), and LaRita. Two years later Emily decided to buy a condo and liked Sarah enough to let her move in. Hiring movers was still one of the best decisions they ever made. Although they no longer live together, Emily and Sarah are still regularly found eating gelato (or frozen yogurt), wandering Lincoln Avenue, or enjoying some beverages on a porch, often tolerating Mark tagging along.
